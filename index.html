Operations

+
Operations · Commercial performance

By the time revenue changes, something else usually changed first.

Every month ended with a number. The useful work happened earlier, in the small changes that showed where the operation had started to drift.

What I watched

Production, attendance, delivery, sales and guest follow-through

A photographer producing fewer sessions, unfinished deliveries or guests missing appointments could look separate. Together, they explained why a target was becoming harder to reach.

How I worked

Start with what falls outside the norm

I reviewed priorities, pending issues, events, updated targets and anything likely to disrupt the day. The goal was to resolve the unusual first, then set the commercial focus for the team.

Scale

USD 40K–60K in monthly revenue targets achieved

Managed resort photography operations handling roughly 200–250 guest transactions each month across three international assignments.

Pattern

Low production

Fewer guest approaches created fewer sessions, leaving the sales problem visible only after the opportunity had already been lost.

Response

Adjust the mechanism

Sales approaches, coupons, staffing focus and daily priorities changed according to the variable causing the gap.

Result

Problems became diagnosable

Instead of stopping at “we are behind,” the operation could ask whether the cause was capture, ticket value, volume or follow-through.

Leadership
Making the system visible.
+
Leadership · Cross-functional work

At first, I thought managing people meant telling them what to do.

The harder part was creating enough clarity for people to do the work without waiting for someone else to make it happen.

The team

Photographers were the center of the operation

About half of my attention went to whether they were producing, selling, reporting and following the standard. When they were not, the useful question was what they lacked.

The system

Guests experienced one result built by many people

Weddings, concierges, hotel departments, corporate teams and photographers all affected the same delivery, often on different timelines.

What changed

Jamaica made authority insufficient

Cultural differences made it clear that people needed a reason to follow, a clear role in the operation and a manager willing to acknowledge mistakes.

Recruitment

Built the team when needed

Started recruitment campaigns, interviewed candidates, trained new hires and handled onboarding.

Coordination

Kept moving parts visible

Handled wedding changes, sick leave, hotel conflicts, delivery backlogs and service escalations while the day continued.

Commercial recovery

Resolved the issue without losing the sale

Guest complaints sometimes ended in four-figure purchases once the concern, service limit and desired outcome were clear.

Systems
Not starting from zero.
+
Process design · Enablement

Before the spreadsheet, monthly planning lived in pencil on a memo pad.

Each month meant rebuilding formulas, remembering assumptions and starting again. I wanted the next month to begin with what the previous one had already taught me.

Planning system

One view connected the variables behind revenue

Occupancy, contacts, sessions, capture rate, average ticket and sales could be translated into monthly, weekly and daily targets, then adjusted as the month changed.

Recurring work

Knowledge moved out of memory

Daily photographer checklists, wedding interview templates, camera settings and editing presets reduced the need to remember or rebuild the same solution.

What it changed

“We are behind” became a question with an answer

The operation could identify whether the gap came from sales volume, ticket value, capture, production or another variable and adjust the plan before the month ended.

Monthly planning

Located the variable behind the gap before setting the next commercial priority.

Occupancy · Production · Capture · Average ticket · Revenue

Weekly adjustment

Translated the monthly target into decisions that could still change the result.

Progress · Gap · Priority · Action · Follow-up

Daily execution

Made priorities, responsibilities and exceptions visible before the day became harder.

Staffing · Events · Sessions · Issues · Next day

Representation
When the image misses the place.
+
Hospitality · Brand · Photography

The resorts were memorable. Their online presence often was not.

I kept arriving at beautiful properties, then opening their websites and social channels and finding a version that missed what made the place worth remembering.

The pattern

Different properties became difficult to distinguish online

The problem appeared across countries and brands. The photographs could be correct while still failing to communicate the experience.

The guest

Representation changed what people were willing to see

Guests who said they hated being photographed often bought hundreds or thousands of dollars in images after the session helped them recognize themselves differently.

The work now

That observation became EXIF Studio

EXIF studies the distance between what a place is and what a guest can perceive before booking.

Practice

Research
Finding what does not fit.
+
Research · Strategy · Writing

Something usually stops making sense before I know why.

A button does not work. A paragraph says something adjacent to what it should say. A photograph feels uncomfortable. The first useful response is usually: what, exactly, is wrong here?

Starting point

Begin with the outcome

I define what the work needs to make possible, then test what prevents the current version from getting there.

Standard

“Sufficient” is not useful when the meaning is still unclear

I keep working when the message, interface, photograph or system leaves room for the wrong interpretation.

Independent evidence

The same method appears across different formats

EXIF research, Observation 01, operational playbooks, application systems and writing frameworks all began with an outcome that the first version could not yet produce.

Research

Find what is actually happening

Separate the observable problem from the first explanation attached to it.

Writing

Make the intended meaning easier to reach

Remove the language that sounds complete while leaving the point unclear.

Direction

Move the work toward a defined outcome

Clarity comes from knowing what the reader, guest or team needs to understand next.
