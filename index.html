Commercial Case · Resort Sales & Operations

Building a Commercial Operating System.

A practical case drawn from progressive responsibility across resort properties in Mexico and the Caribbean. The work combined direct consultative sales, KPI management, team development, recruitment and cross-functional coordination.

200 to 2000+ usddirect transaction range
40000 to 60000 usdmonthly team revenue supported
250+monthly team transactions
6resort properties

Context

I progressed from resort photographer to team lead, operations manager and senior operations and sales manager within one continuous hospitality program. That progression required more than selling. It required building repeatable systems around people, performance and service delivery.

Commercial challenge

The operation depended on consistent guest capture, strong consultative selling, disciplined follow-up and reliable daily execution. Results were affected by team capability, guest volume, operational timing, hotel coordination and the ability to identify where performance was breaking down.

What I built

Upsell structure

A tiered framework that connected transaction value, package logic and daily production goals.

KPI system

Weekly tracking around revenue, average ticket, guest capture, booked sessions and individual performance.

Performance tool

A custom Google Sheets analysis system for weekly reviews, monthly planning and leadership reporting.

Team progression

A four-level development framework with onboarding benchmarks, bilateral performance logs and clearer evaluation cadence.

Operational scope

I recruited and onboarded new hires, coached performance, handled formal warnings and separations, and coordinated with Events, F&B, Guest Services, hotel management and legal contacts. The goal was to protect both revenue and service quality while keeping the operation usable for the team on the ground.

Why it matters

This experience translates directly to hospitality SaaS, account management, customer success, partnerships and commercial operations. I understand hotel teams as users, the pressure behind revenue goals and the difference between a process that looks good in a presentation and one that people can actually follow during a busy shift.

Case 01 · Hotel Visual Reset

Table First.

Self-initiated visual strategy exercise for a boutique Relais & Châteaux property in Saint Lucia. 49 rooms, two restaurants, private beach, Relais & Châteaux member, Forbes Best Boutique Hotel Saint Lucia 2025 and Condé Nast Top 40 Caribbean Resorts 2025. Property identity withheld by request. Original photography taken on location.

Problem

The property has a genuinely distinct identity in its architecture, gastronomy and scale, but its visual communication does not yet give a prospective guest a reason to feel something before they book. The imagery documents the place without revealing the experience of being in it.

Creative concept

Table First. The visual system builds outward from the gastronomic experience, which is the property’s clearest differentiator. Three restaurants, three distinct atmospheres. The territory is anticipation: the moment before the plate arrives, the light changing, the glass already poured.

EXIF Studio · Independent practice

Brand and website design for a hospitality practice.

EXIF Studio is a self-directed brand and website project built to translate a hospitality point of view into a clear visual identity. The work covers positioning, naming, brand language, identity development, website structure, interaction decisions and the design system behind the final site.

Design approach

The project began with positioning, then moved into a restrained identity system built around observation, detail and visual judgment. From there, I defined the website architecture, page hierarchy, editorial rhythm, image behavior, typography, color system and concise brand voice. The goal was not to make the site look like a hotel. It was to make the thinking behind the practice visible.

Brand strategy

Positioning, audience, naming logic, verbal direction and a clear distinction from generic hospitality marketing language.

Identity system

The EXIF eye, mark variations, usage rules, restrained color palette and a visual language built around observation.

Website architecture

Page hierarchy, navigation, case-study structure and an editorial system designed to keep the work concise and legible.

Interface direction

Typography, spacing, image sequencing, transitions and responsive behavior developed as one consistent digital experience.

Independent project founded and directed by Katia Pmtzk. Visit exif.studio.
