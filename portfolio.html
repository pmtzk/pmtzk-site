Brand Identity · Web Design · Service Design · 2025

EXIF Studio

The brief I wrote for myself: build a studio that serves boutique hotels the way a creative director would — not as a photographer who shows up and shoots, but as someone who understands how a property earns its rate and knows what imagery needs to do before it can be beautiful.

Every decision in the brand came from the same question: what communicates precision without coldness? The eye mark draws from EXIF's meaning — the invisible metadata layer that gives images context. Five logo variations designed for different applications, from favicon to photo watermark to embossed stationery. One restraint rule throughout: never decorate what you can earn.

The website was built from scratch in HTML/CSS, deployed via Cloudflare Pages and GitHub, bilingual from launch. Service architecture built around two entry points: the Atmosphere Audit and the Visual Reset — sold as projects with defined scope, not retainers.

Web Design · Personal Branding · 2025

pmtzk.co

The constraint: a site that reads as professional without reading as a portfolio. A hospitality operator in active job search doesn't show personality the way a designer does — she shows track record, clarity of thought and commercial credibility.

Built from scratch in HTML/CSS with no framework, deployed on Cloudflare Pages from a GitHub repo. Designed around Cormorant Garamond and Inter — a pair that mirrors the tone of the work: warm and precise in the same breath. Every section anticipates a question a hiring manager would have before they ask it. Metrics visible immediately, no content between them and the contact.

The site is actively used in applications to roles in luxury travel, hotel technology and destination management across Mexico and the Caribbean.

Art Direction · Editorial Design · Publication Architecture · 2026

The Digital Society

The idea starts with a real problem: people with good taste have stopped enjoying their own taste because they spend all their time managing it. The Digital Society is a monthly publication for people who want to return to an atmosphere rather than build one.

Each volume is a complete atmosphere — color systems, editorial writing, curated film, music, book and object lists, downloadable wallpapers in 4K. The design language lives in the tension between moody richness (deep crimson, liquid gold, near-black) and quiet typographic precision. The challenge was designing artifacts that feel valuable before the content is read — objects, not documents.

Volume 001 — Wine Atelier — is complete: 10 wallpapers optimized for iPad, desktop and phone, with a typographic system and voice guide for the editorial layer. Membership architecture and back-catalog strategy finalized.

Systems Design · Team Development · Operations · 2025–2026

PhotoPro Operating System

The problem: a team with no shared language for what good looks like. New photographers arrived and left on the same timeline, with no documentation of why either happened. Performance conversations had no anchors. Evaluation happened only when something went wrong.

The solution was a full career progression system — four levels (Stage, Lifestyle, Lifestyle+, Lead) with defined competencies at each stage, bilateral progress logbooks evaluated on a set cadence, and parallel specialization tracks for weddings and mentorship. The design challenge was translating operational knowledge into a legible system: one that a new manager could inherit and run, and that a photographer could see themselves inside of.

Implemented with a team of 5+ across two property rotations. Adopted by regional direction as the documentation standard for the market.

System Design Documentation Training Architecture Performance Management Notion Operations
Stage
Foundation — technique, service standards, consistency. The entry level for all new photographers regardless of prior experience.
Lifestyle
Guest-read capability, editorial eye, sales fluency. Can work independently and contribute to daily revenue targets.
Lifestyle+
Specialization track — weddings or mentorship. Higher-value sessions, junior team support, event leadership.
Lead
Full operational ownership, team management, recruitment cycle. The system was designed to develop this role from within.
